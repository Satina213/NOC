<HTML>

HTML stands for Hyper Text Markup Language and it's used to organize the structure of webpages. It's a hierarchical structure whereby each element will belong to some parent. Look at the example on the right. The head and body are both the children of HTML. Within the head and body there are more elements.
The head of the html document will contain information about the page but is not the content of the page. Included here is the charset declaring which alphabet is being used, the viewport is modified so on mobile images will scale well, the title of the page as seen at the tab on the top of the screen, and a link to the style sheet. 


    <!DOCTYPE html>
    <html lang="en">
    <head>
        <meta charset="UTF-8">
        <meta name="viewport" content="width=device-width, initial-scale=1.0">
        <title> Put your title here </title>
        <link rel="stylesheet" href="styles.css">
    </head>
    <body>
        <p> content goes here </p>
    </body>
So what sorts of tags can we use in our html body to change the appearance or layout of information? Let's start big: with headers.
